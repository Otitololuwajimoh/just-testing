What is Economics?

Economics is the study of how people allocate scarce resources to meet their needs and desires. It explores how individuals, businesses, and governments make decisions about resource allocation, production, and consumption.

Key Concepts

  • Supply and Demand: The relationship between the availability of a good or service and the demand for it, which determines its price.
  • Opportunity Cost: The cost of forgoing the next best alternative when making a decision.
  • Elasticity: The responsiveness of the quantity demanded or supplied of a good to a change in its price.
  • Market Equilibrium: The point at which the quantity of a good supplied equals the quantity demanded.

Basic Economic Principles

Economics is governed by a few core principles that influence the behavior of individuals and institutions:

  • Scarcity: Limited resources vs. unlimited wants.
  • Rational Behavior: People make decisions to maximize their satisfaction.
  • Incentives: People respond to changes in incentives, such as prices or rewards.
